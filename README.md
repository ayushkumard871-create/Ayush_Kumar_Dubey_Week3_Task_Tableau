# HR Analytics Dashboard — Attrition & Performance (Tableau)

Brief Summary:
Interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing employee attrition, performance trends, and demographic segmentation to identify workforce risk factors and improve retention strategy.


---

📌 Overview

This project focuses on workforce analytics using Tableau to examine employee attrition patterns and performance indicators. The objective was to transform HR data into a structured, decision-support dashboard that helps leadership understand retention risks, workforce distribution, and performance concentration.

The project emphasizes data storytelling — not just visualization — by connecting employee demographics, job roles, compensation, and performance ratings to attrition behavior.


---

❓ Problem Statement

Employee attrition directly impacts recruitment cost, training investment, productivity, and organizational stability.

However, companies often struggle to answer:

Which departments experience the highest attrition?

Are certain age groups or demographics more likely to leave?

Does salary level influence retention?

Is there a relationship between performance ratings and attrition?

Where should HR focus retention efforts?


This project aims to uncover those patterns through structured dashboard analysis.

---

💡 Key Insights

Attrition was concentrated within specific departments and job roles.

Younger employees and those with shorter tenure showed higher turnover rates.

Compensation disparities appeared linked to retention trends in some segments.

High performers leaving represent a strategic risk beyond simple headcount loss.

Certain roles experienced structural retention issues, not random exits.

📈 Results & Conclusions

The analysis demonstrates that attrition is not random — it follows identifiable patterns across departments, tenure levels, and compensation bands.

By using interactive storytelling in Tableau, the dashboard enables HR leadership to:

Identify high-risk employee segments

Allocate retention resources more effectively

Address structural issues rather than surface-level symptoms


This project reflects the ability to connect workforce metrics to strategic organizational outcomes.
